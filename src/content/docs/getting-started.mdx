Orchard is an all-in-one manager for [Cashu](https://cashu.space) mints. It
gives you a single place to set up, operate, and keep watch over your own
mint — the **groundskeeper** for your sovereign bank in cyberspace.

This guide orients you before you install. As the project's first release
stabilizes, the command-level steps will land right here.

<Aside type="tip" title="Brand new?">
  Cashu is a free, open-source Chaumian ecash protocol for Bitcoin — private,
  instant, low-fee digital cash with no accounts and no surveillance. If that's
  unfamiliar, skim [cashu.space](https://cashu.space) first; the rest of these
  docs assume that mental model.
</Aside>

## What Orchard does

A Cashu mint issues and redeems ecash backed by a Lightning wallet. Running one
well means watching balances, keys, and proofs; reacting when something needs
attention; and doing it without babysitting a terminal. Orchard wraps that work
in one manager:

- **Operate your mint** — keys, balances, and mint info from a single dashboard.
- **Stay informed** — health and activity surfaced where you already are.
- **Tend, don't babysit** — the groundskeepers watch the mint so you don't have
  to refresh a log all day.

## What you'll need

<Steps>

1. **A Cashu mint to manage.** Orchard manages a mint — it doesn't replace one.
   You'll point it at your mint's backend.

2. **A Lightning backend.** Cashu mints settle against Lightning, so you'll need
   a node or service your mint can reach.

3. **Somewhere to run it.** Orchard is self-hosted: a VPS, a home server, or any
   box you control and can keep online.

</Steps>
