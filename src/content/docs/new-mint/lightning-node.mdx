The settlement layer. Your Cashu mint issues ecash backed by Lightning, so this
node is what moves real sats in and out when users mint and melt. It anchors to
the Bitcoin node you set up in the previous step.

This guide uses [LND](https://github.com/lightningnetwork/lnd), Lightning Labs'
widely deployed node. Your mint connects to it over gRPC/REST with a macaroon and
TLS certificate.

## What this step covers

- **The node**, pointed at your Bitcoin Core node's RPC and notification endpoints.
- **A funded channel or two**: outbound liquidity to melt, inbound to mint.
- **API access** your mint can authenticate against in the next step.

## Follow MiniBolt's Lightning guide

[MiniBolt's Lightning section](https://minibolt.minibolt.info/lightning/lightning)
is the step-by-step we recommend. The first two pages are required for an Orchard
mint; the apps after them are optional:

- **[3.1 Lightning client: LND](https://minibolt.minibolt.info/lightning/lightning/lightning-client)** (follow):
  Install and configure LND on top of your Bitcoin node.
- **[3.2 Channel backup for LND](https://minibolt.minibolt.info/lightning/lightning/channel-backup)** (follow):
  Set up static channel backups. Do not skip this. It is how you recover funds if
  the node is lost.
- **[3.3 Web app: ThunderHub](https://minibolt.minibolt.info/lightning/lightning/web-app)** (optional, recommended):
  A web channel manager for LND. Orchard does not have channel management yet, so
  run ThunderHub (or a similar manager) in the meantime.
- **[3.4 Mobile app: Zeus](https://minibolt.minibolt.info/lightning/lightning/mobile-app)** (optional):
  A phone app for keeping an eye on your node.

<Aside type="note" title="Keep the credentials handy">
  Your mint authenticates to LND with its TLS certificate and a macaroon, pointed at
  the node's gRPC or REST endpoint. Note where those live: you hand them to the mint
  in the next step.
</Aside>
