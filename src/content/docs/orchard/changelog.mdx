The change log is Orchard's record of what's happened across your stack — a running
timeline of events you can scroll back through. When something looks off on the
[dashboard](/orchard/dashboard/), this is where you find out what led up to it.

## Entries

Each entry records who did what, where, and when:

- **User** — the actor behind the event: a crew member, the **System**, or an AI **Agent**.
- **Section** — which service it touched: Bitcoin, Lightning, Mint, Ecash, AI, or Settings.
- **Event** — what happened, like a setting updated, a keyset created, or an agent executed.
- **Time** — when it occurred.

Expand a row to see the **details** — each field that changed, its value before and after,
and whether the change came through as **Success** or **Error**.

## Timeline

Above the table, a chart plots events across the range you're viewing. Each bubble groups
the events in a slice of time, sized by how many it holds and coloured by how they went —
all succeeded, all failed, or a mix. Hover one for the count and the breakdown.

## Filtering

The log grows quickly, so you can narrow it down. Set a **date range** (it starts on the
last 30 days), then filter by **section**, **event type** (created, updated, deleted,
executed), **status** (success, error, partial), or **user**. Page through the results and
choose how many to show at a time.
